#  UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into transferring money through digital payment applications.

In a typical situation, a fraudster contacts the victim pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or an online buyer. The victim is asked to approve a payment request or share an OTP, claiming that it is required to receive money. However, this action actually results in money being sent from the victim’s account to the fraudster.

Students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use mobile payment apps and may not always be aware of such scams. Fraudsters usually create urgency so that the victim reacts quickly without verifying the request.

The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal information. In many cases, the money cannot be recovered easily.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful behaviour while using digital payment platforms.
